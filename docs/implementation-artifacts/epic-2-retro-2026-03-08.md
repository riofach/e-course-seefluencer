## Deep Story Analysis Synthesis

### Common Struggles

- Story 2.2 and Story 2.3 depended heavily on preserving Story 2.1's baseline correctly.
- Search behavior required careful handling of debounce, URL state, wildcard escaping, and published-only filtering.
- Next.js App Router details remained a sensitive implementation area, especially around Suspense, async params, and cache behavior.

### Recurring Review Feedback

- Keep query and business logic in `src/server/courses/` rather than in page/client components.
- Preserve the architecture decisions: RSC-first reads, URL search params as state, and no unnecessary REST routes.
- Maintain accessibility and UX consistency through skeletons, empty states, semantic structure, and focus/touch targets.
- Prefer extending existing types, mappers, and UI patterns instead of creating duplicate paths.

### Breakthrough Moments

- Story 2.1 created a reusable public catalog foundation that accelerated Stories 2.2 and 2.3.
- Story 2.2 made search shareable and bookmarkable through URL-driven state without breaking the server-rendered model.
- Story 2.3 completed the discovery funnel and turned Epic 2 into a coherent user journey.

### Testing and Quality Insights

- Test evidence exists in `node:test` coverage around helpers, queries, and mapping logic.
- Validation discipline consistently included test, lint, and typecheck steps.
- Manual testing remained important for slug flow, search behavior, detail rendering, and syllabus validation.
